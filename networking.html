What I Covered In This Module

In this module, I have covered the fundamentals of designing and implementing computer networks based on the needs of a business. This includes understanding network topologies, protocols, and security measures. I have also learned how to configure network devices and Windows server systems to provide required functionality. We also explored the importance of network protocols within the TCP/IP stack and the OSI model, and how they facilitate communication between devices.

Examples of Work

Within my assignments for this module, I have applied the knowledge I have gained to simulate a network for an organisation within Cisco Packet Tracer. This involved designing a network topology that meets the requirements of the business, configuring network devices such as routers and switches, and implementing security measures to protect the network. I was also tasked with planning, designing and demonstrating a simple network for a business, which included creating network diagrams, configuring network devices and configuring a Windows server.
